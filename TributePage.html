The most important dates of Nelson Mandela

  • 1918 ~ Mandela was born on 18 July.
  • 1933 ~ Mandela began his secondary education at Clarkebury Methodist High School in Engcobo, a Western-style institution that was the largest school for black Africans in Thembuland. Made to socialise with other students on an equal basis, he claimed that he lost his "stuck up" attitude, becoming best friends with a girl for the first time.
  • 1940 ~ He found that Jongintaba had arranged marriages for him and Justice; dismayed, they fled to Johannesburg via Queenstown, arriving in April 1941. He found work as a night watchman at Crown Mines, his "first sight of South African capitalism in action", but was fired when the induna (headman) discovered that he was a runaway.
  • 1943 ~ He returned to Johannesburg to follow a political path as a lawyer rather than become a privy councillor in Thembuland. He later stated that he experienced no epiphany, but that he "simply found [himself] doing so, and could not do otherwise.
  • 1950 ~ Mandela took Xuma's place on the ANC national executive in March 1950, and that same year was elected national president of the ANCYL.
  • 1961 ~ Inspired by the actions of Fidel Castro's 26th of July Movement in the Cuban Revolution, in 1961 Mandela, Sisulu, and Slovo co-founded Umkhonto we Sizwe ("Spear of the Nation", abbreviated MK).
  • 1962 ~ Police captured Mandela along with fellow activist Cecil Williams near Howick On 5 August 1962.
  • 1964 ~ Mandela and his co-accused were transferred from Pretoria to the prison on Robben Island, remaining there for the next 18 years.
  • 1988 ~ Mandela was moved to Victor Verster Prison near Paarl in December 1988. He was housed in the relative comfort of a warder's house with a personal cook, and used the time to complete his LLB degree. While there, he was permitted many visitors and organised secret communications with exiled ANC leader Oliver Tambo.
  • 1994 ~ He took the Nobel Peace Prize.
  • 1994 ~ Mandela headed a Government of National Unity dominated by the ANC.
  • 1999 ~ He went for retirement.
  • 2004 ~ Johannesburg granted Mandela the Freedom of the City, in 2004.
  • 2013 ~ Mandela died in Johannesburg, agged 95.
